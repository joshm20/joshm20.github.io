Coursework

Computer Science

In my time at JMU, I've had the opportunity to interact and code with multiple languages. In CS149: Introduction to Programming, I coded with Python in Thonny IDE to learn basic programming ideas. After that, in CS159: Advanced Programming, I coded in Java, and started to work in Eclipse. I was introduced to object-orientated programming ideas and things like unit testing, recursion, and project desing. Additionally, I used JUnit tests to perform automated testing on project requirements.
In sophomore year, I took CS240: Data Structures and Algorithms and CS345: Software Engieneering. CS240 was the standard DSA class in things like arrays, queues, stacks, trees, sorting, searching, etc. CS345 introduced industry topics like development process, continous integration, agile methods, quality assurance, testing, and design principles. At the end of the semester, we practiced the Scrum methodology to create a movie searching database similar to IMDb.
After, I took CS261: Computer Systems I, which taught me programming in C and working in Unix environments. It also built up computer systems from binary encodings, up through Assembly, machine architecture, and operating systems in general.

Mathematics

Starting out with credit for Calculus 1 and 2, I first took Math 237: Calculus 3. The next semester, in pursuit of a minor, I took Math 245: Discrete Mathematics and Math 300: Linear Algebra, two classes that introduced me to mathematical proofs. It wasn't until after these two classes that I actually declared a major in Mathematics. (Math was too interesting to stop after these).
Following this, I took Math 430/431: Abstract Algebra 1 and 2, which I found especially satisfying and opened me up to the space of higher mathematics and math research. During this time, I also took Math 475: Fundamental Concepts of Geometry. This class axiomatically built an understanding of geometry straight from Euclid's Elements, followed by delving into some non-euclidean concepts, the upper-half plane being one example. After that, I took MATH 485: Topics: Cryptography, which explored the mathematics behind cryptography. Specifically, RSA, square roots mod n, primality testing, discrete logarithms, elliptic curve cryptography, and hash functions.
